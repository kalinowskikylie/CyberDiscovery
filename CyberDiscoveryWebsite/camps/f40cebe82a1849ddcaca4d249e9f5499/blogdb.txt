<p>There is a simple closed-loop course set up in MCST 103. Your mission&commasepar; should you choose to accept it&commasepar; is to program your BOE-Bot to navigate the course. The rules are simple: you will start at the designated spot&commasepar; travel around the established landmark&commasepar; and return the Bot to its original starting point.</p>
<p>You can travel in whichever direction you choose&commasepar; and how you map your path is up to you. There are no time constraints. The only measure of success will be if your Bot begins and ends at the same point (after circling the landmark&commasepar; of course).</p>
<p>The course will only be open for practice during Lunch and Team Time. When you are ready&commasepar; you may ask any of the CyberDiscovery Team members to verify your success. If/when you complete this optional challenge&commasepar; your team will be awarded 20 points. There is no bonus for being first&commasepar; and no penalty for being last (unless&commasepar; of course&commasepar; you don't do it at all&commasepar; in which case you simply get no points).</p>
<p>While there's no bonus for finishing first&commasepar; once you complete this challenge you will be permitted to choose your slot in the queue for the Maze Challenge. So&commasepar; if you finish this first&commasepar; you can decide whether you want to go first or last (or wherever in the middle) on the maze. If you finish this last&commasepar; you will be stuck with whatever position in the queue is still open. If you have not signed onto the queue by 5:00PM&commasepar; you will be placed on the queue in whatever open spot remains.</p>

<li><strong>Size:</strong> Your fort must fit entirely on the designated Battle Station tiles. The battle arena will have regularly spaced battle stations&commasepar; denoted by gray tiles. Each Battle Station is exactly 24 inches wide by 12 inches deep.</li>

<p>If you are driving along&commasepar; tapping in commands&commasepar; going forward&commasepar; spinning in circles&commasepar; whatever&commasepar; and it's so exciting that you are holding the "w" key down for a solid minute. <p>If you bought a hippo jaw from the auction&commasepar; wise choice! Also&commasepar; you will need to program your jaw just a bit differently than the putter. It raises and lowers like the putter&commasepar; but the jaws use a standard servo&commasepar; where the putters (and the wheels as well) use continuous servos.</p>
<p>A standard servo operates in a similar manner to a continuous servo with one major difference. When sent a pulse&commasepar; a standard servo will move to an absolute position. A continuous servo moves to a relative position. This means that the continuous servo rotates an entire 360&deg; (which is what you need for your wheels)&commasepar; but the standard only has a 180&deg; rotation (which is actually perfect for just up-and-down motion).</p>
<p>The range of pulse widths that are valid for a standard servo is 375 to 1125. Each pulse in this range corresponds to a position between 0 and 180 degrees.
